Victory Square

Victory Square in Minsk is one of the most revered and solemn memorial complexes, dedicated to the people's feat in the Great Patriotic War.

In its center stands a 40-meter obelisk, crowned with the image of the Order of Victory, and at its base burns the Eternal Flame in memory of the fallen heroes.

Beneath the square lies a spacious underground memorial hall, whose walls immortalize the names of the Heroes of the Soviet Union who gave their lives for the liberation of Belarus.

This place serves as the main venue for memorial events on Victory Day and symbolizes the eternal memory and deep gratitude to the defenders of the Fatherland.

Island of Tears

Island of Tears is a memorial dedicated to the Belarusian soldiers who perished during the war in Afghanistan.

This monument is situated on an artificial island in the Svislach River, in the center of Minsk.

At the heart of the memorial stands a chapel with sculptures of grieving mothers.

The atmosphere here is deeply moving, encouraging visitors to reflect on the cost of war.

Crying Guardian Angel

The "Crying Guardian Angel" is an emotional bronze sculpture located near the Island of Tears in Minsk.

The angel is depicted with his head bowed, and a tear streaming down his cheek, creating a poignant image of grief and eternal memory.

This monument serves as a guardian of the souls of fallen warriors and embodies the sorrow of all who lost loved ones in war.

The sculpture evokes deep emotions in visitors, making them reflect on the price of peace and the fragility of human life.

"City Scales" Sculpture

The "City Scales" Sculpture is a modern art installation in the form of large scales, located in one of Minsk's central districts.

This original monument symbolizes the balance and justice that should form the foundation of urban life and governance.

Through the metaphor of scales, the creators convey the idea of equilibrium between different aspects of a modern city's development.

The interactive sculpture attracts attention from both residents and visitors of the capital, becoming a popular spot for photography.
